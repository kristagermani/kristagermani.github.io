Exterior

Since I got to design most of the inside of the home, I wanted Robert to pic out the exterior color palette. He actually chose the exact color scheme I liked best! He went with dark colors -- which was nice along with the interior -- and a beautiful bright red door.

When building a home, you have to choose priorities. We did prioritize most of our "splurge" items for the inside of the house, but the other dream item we both would have gone for would have been a front porch. We figured the deck in the back yard would make up for that, though!

Some of Robert's priorities when it came to the exterior of the home were:

  • Having a nice space to grill
  • Having a big seating area so we can host cookouts
  • Getting a front porch to sit and have coffee on

As I mentioned before, we did have to work with our budget, so we did not end up getting a front porch, but we can still sit side by side on the little concrete space and enjoy one another's company there!

Building the Exterior

The total build time of the exterior took about 3 months. They are still putting the finishing touches on the house, but I will go ahead and show some progress photos from weekly or bimonthly updates.

One huge benefit of building a home in a new neighborhood, rather than on your own plot of land, is that you lock in at a price and keep it, whether or not lumber prices go up. This ended up being a huge blessing for us, as lumber prices were crazy this year!

Our Land

When you purchase a plat of land in a neighborhood, your home obviously will take up a good portion of it. We got the smallest home available, (fun fact: the three windows on the bottom right are a garage, not part of the house!) which does mean that we get a bigger yard than we would have if we had more square footage in our home.

We also had to have our land leveled according to what would be good with the county. This is to decrease the risk of flooding or other issues in our home in the future. We have a plat guide that shows all of the levels of our land.
